    Our Story

    Started in 2020, the Gaia Project is an organization focusing on providing equal opportunity for women in the workspace around the world. We believe that building a more inclusive workspace is an essential part of sustaining business growth.

    The entrepreneurial landscape shows a marked underrepresentation of women founders, who also encounter lower levels of venture capital investment compared average. By raising awareness for gender equity, creating spaces for collaboration, and amplifying female presence, we provide tailored resources and support to empower female entrepreneurs to thrive and reshape the business landscape worldwide.

    Our Name

    "The Gaia Project" takes its name from the Earth goddess Gaia. In ancient Greek, "Gaia: Γαῖα" is a poetic expression of the Earth. The Earth goddess Gaia is the embodiment of nature, the mother of life, and the soul of the Earth.

    Our Mission

    We are committed to foster the global landscape for women entrepreneurs. By providing business incubation and legal consultation, amplifying traditionally underrepresented voices, and enhancing women’s access to diverse resources, we empower underrepresented voices to become pivotal players in the startup ecosystem. Collaborating closely with founders and change-makers around the world, Gaia nurtures international growth of women’s presence in venture, propelling transformative changes in the venture ecosystem.

    Our Vision

    To have 30% of the world’s venture capital to be invested in startups led/found by women and gender nonbinary individuals. This data is 2% in 2024. Only 2% venture capital were used to invest in women founders.

    Fellowship Program

    Entrepreneurship Mentor Initiative
    • Collaborate with startups predominantly founded by women, providing volunteer opportunities that bridge the gap between talented individuals and female founders.
    • Form cohort partnerships and collaborative groups among women founders and individuals passionate about women's entrepreneurship.
    • Partner with enterprises to provide startups with comprehensive services in legal affairs, financing, and compliance, etc.
    • Foster environments that facilitate collaboration and dialogue among entrepreneurs.
    • Jointly develop programs that empower female entrepreneurs.

    New York, NY

    New York University

    • Gaia Club members at NYU meet at least once a month to share recent thoughts and ideas, collaboratively producing op-ed articles.
    • Each meeting has a predetermined theme, and members are invited to gather their thoughts on different topics.
    • Following our discussions, NYU partners compile and publish monthly articles to share our insights with subscribers.

    Ann Arbor, MI

    University of Michigan Ann Arbor

    • The Gaia Project at UMich is an independent non-profit organization that campaigns for environmental protection and gender equity by advocating for real-world solutions to sustainability, equity, and equality.
    • The organization provides an equal and open platform for communication for all students and the broader community, aiming to promote a more diverse and inclusive social environment.

    Seattle, WA

    University of Washington

    • Our Gaia members consist of independent eco-feminists dedicated to creating a better ecological and gender environment. We advocate for environmental protection and gender equity by promoting practical solutions for sustainability and equality.
    • To encourage diversity at UW, we aim to develop a platform that unites students from all majors and ethnicities who are passionate about environmental and gender equity issues.
